About me

My name is Hoa, currently a junior at Lehigh University with a major in Computer Science and Business and a minor in Entrepreneurship.

One of the first classes I took at Lehigh - Data Structure and Object-Oriented Programming - excited me at every lecture. The course reaffirmed my passion in programming and the big picture of how data are designed, stored, managed, and connected. To me, the best part about programming is to break down problems, analyze each subproblems, and use algorithms to figure out “a” best answer to solve them. I enjoy managing data to ensure preventions of data leakage and false logic.

In 2018, to explore the tech world beyond schooling, I spent a gap year to intern as a software engineer at several companies. There, since I kept asking questions regarding data storage and security management while coding, my mentor recommended that I try out network security. Taking the advice, I self-studied network security with the Certified Ethical Hacking Program and discovered my new interest. In the near future, my goal will be to improve my software engineering and cybersecurity knowledge by having hands-on experience and learning from others whether it is at school, at work, or at Hackathons.

Graduating in May 2021, I would like to work in the field of software engineering and cybersecurity. If you have any recommendations, advice, or would want to reach out to me for opportunities, please find me at email htb320@lehigh.edu. Thank you!
